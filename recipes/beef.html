Beef Stew Recipe

Beef stew is a hearty, comforting dish perfect for a cozy meal. It’s packed with tender beef, vegetables, and a flavorful broth

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs (900 g) beef chuck, cut into 1-inch cubes
  • 2 tbsp olive oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups beef broth (or 1 quart)
  • 1 cup red wine (optional; you can substitute with extra beef broth)
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tsp dried thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 4 large carrots, peeled and sliced into rounds
  • 3 large potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 2 cups frozen peas (or fresh if in season)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 tbsp chopped fresh parsley (optional, for garnish)/li>

Steps:

    Brown the Beef:

  1. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the beef cubes in batches (don’t overcrowd the pot) and brown them on all sides. This should take about 5-7 minutes per batch. Remove the beef and set aside.

    3. Cook the Aromatics:

  3. In the same pot, add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the minced garlic and cook for an additional 1 minute.

    5. Deglaze the Pot:

  5. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  6. If using red wine, pour it in now, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Allow it to simmer for a few minutes until slightly reduced.

    7. Build the Stew:

  7. Return the browned beef to the pot. Sprinkle the flour over the beef and stir to coat. Cook for 2 minutes.
  8. Add the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, dried thyme, and bay leaves. Stir to combine.

    9. Simmer:

  9. Bring the stew to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and let it simmer for about 1.5 hours, or until the beef is tender.

    10. Add Vegetables:

  10. Add the carrots and potatoes. Continue to simmer, covered, for another 30-40 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.

    11. Finish the Stew:

  11. Stir in the frozen peas (or fresh peas if using). Cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  12. Remove the bay leaves. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

    13. Garnish and Serve:

  13. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley if desired. Serve hot with crusty bread or over a bed of rice.
